Maize

Zea mays

Cereal

Origin

Mesoamerica

Yield Potential

3-8 tons/ha

Startup Capital

$500 - $1,200/ha

Revenue Potential

$1,500 - $4,000/ha

Importance

Food Security

Maize is a staple food for over 300 million Africans, providing 30% of calories consumed.

Industrial Uses

Used for animal feed, starch, corn oil, ethanol and other industrial products.

Cultivation Guide

Land Preparation

  1. Clear land and remove debris
  2. Plow to 15-20cm depth
  3. Harrow to achieve fine tilth
  4. Apply 5-10 tons/ha of manure (optional)

Planting

Seed Selection

  • Use certified seeds (hybrid or OPV)
  • Recommended varieties: WH507, WH505, Oba 98, Sammaz series
  • Seed rate: 20-25kg/ha

Field Planting

  • Planting density: 50,000-60,000 plants/ha (75x25cm or 50x50cm)
  • Planting depth: 3-5cm
  • Best planted at onset of rains

Maintenance

Weed Control

Critical first 6 weeks. 2-3 manual weedings or use herbicides.

Fertilization

Apply NPK (15-15-15) at 400kg/ha at planting. Topdress with urea at 200kg/ha at 4-5 weeks.

Pest Management

Monitor for stem borers, armyworms, storage weevils. Use resistant varieties.

Harvest & Post-Harvest

Harvesting

  • Maturity: 90-120 days depending on variety
  • Yield: 3-8 tons/ha depending on management
  • Harvest when husks dry and turn brown

Post-Harvest

  • Dry to 12-13% moisture content for storage
  • Shelling can be manual or mechanical
  • Store in hermetic bags or silos to prevent weevil damage
