Cassava

Manihot esculenta

Root Crop

Origin

South America (Domesticated 8,000-10,000 years ago)

Yield Potential

10-40 tons/ha

Startup Capital

$800 - $1,500/ha

Revenue Potential

$2,500 - $10,000/ha

Importance

Food Security

Cassava provides 37% of dietary energy in West Africa. Drought-resistant nature makes it crucial for food security.

Industrial Uses

Processed into gari, fufu, starch, ethanol and animal feed. Nigeria is world's largest cassava producer.

Cultivation Guide

Land Preparation

  1. Clear land manually or with herbicides (3-4 weeks before planting)
  2. Plow to 20-30cm depth (deeper in compacted soils)
  3. Harrow to achieve fine tilth (especially for mechanical planting)
  4. Form mounds or ridges in waterlogged areas
  5. Apply 5-10 tons/ha of manure or compost (optional but beneficial)

Planting

Stem Selection

  • Select healthy stems from 8-18 month old plants
  • Cut stems into 20-25cm stakes with 5-7 nodes
  • Treat with fungicide if disease pressure is high

Field Planting

  • Planting density: 10,000 plants/ha (1x1m) to 12,500 plants/ha (0.8x1m)
  • Plant stakes vertically or at 45Â° angle, 5-10cm deep
  • Best planted at onset of rains (can delay up to 2 months)

Maintenance

Weed Control

Critical first 3-4 months. 2-3 manual weedings or use pre-emergence herbicides.

Fertilization

Apply NPK (12-12-17) at 400-600kg/ha at planting. Topdress at 6-8 weeks if needed.

Pest Management

Monitor for cassava mealybug, green mite. Use resistant varieties like TMS varieties.

Harvest & Post-Harvest

Harvesting

  • Maturity: 8-24 months depending on variety
  • Yield: 10-15 tons/ha (subsistence), 25-40 tons/ha (commercial)
  • Methods: Manual (pull stems) or mechanical harvesters

Post-Harvest

  • Process within 48hrs to prevent deterioration
  • Peeling, washing, grating, fermenting, pressing, sieving, roasting (for gari)
  • Sun drying for chips (3-5 days to 14% moisture)
  • Storage: Processed products store better than fresh roots
