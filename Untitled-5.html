Yam

Dioscorea spp.

Root Crop

Origin

West Africa

Yield Potential

10-25 tons/ha

Startup Capital

$1,000 - $2,000/ha

Revenue Potential

$3,000 - $7,500/ha

Importance

Cultural Significance

Yam is a staple food in West Africa with deep cultural roots, especially in Nigeria, Ghana and Côte d'Ivoire.

Economic Value

West Africa produces over 90% of the world's yam, with Nigeria accounting for 70% of global production.

Cultivation Guide

Land Preparation

  1. Clear land and remove tree stumps
  2. Plow to 30-40cm depth (yam requires deep, loose soil)
  3. Form mounds (1m diameter, 1m high) for better tuber development
  4. Apply 10-15 tons/ha of compost or manure

Planting

Seed Selection

  • Use healthy seed yams (200-500g pieces)
  • Popular varieties: White Guinea Yam, Water Yam, Yellow Yam
  • Treat with fungicide to prevent rot

Field Planting

  • Plant at onset of rains
  • Planting density: 10,000-15,000 plants/ha
  • Plant 10-15cm deep in mounds with cut surface up

Maintenance

Staking

Provide 2m stakes for vines to climb. Improves yield by 30-50%.

Fertilization

Apply NPK (15-15-15) at 400-600kg/ha at planting. Topdress at 6 weeks.

Pest Control

Watch for yam beetles, nematodes. Use resistant varieties and crop rotation.

Harvest & Post-Harvest

Harvesting

  • Maturity: 7-12 months depending on variety
  • Signs: Yellowing leaves, drying vines
  • Yield: 10-15 tons/ha (local), 20-25 tons/ha (improved)

Post-Harvest

  • Cure at 30-35°C and 90% RH for 3-5 days
  • Store in well-ventilated barns (6-8 months shelf life)
  • Process into yam flour, chips or instant pounded yam

Financial Analysis (1 hectare)

Startup Costs

  • Seed yam $400-$800
  • Land Preparation $300-$500
  • Stakes $200-$400
  • Total $1,000-$2,000

Operating Costs

  • Labor $500-$800
  • Fertilizers $200-$300
  • Pest Control $100-$200

Revenue

Yield: 10-25 tons

Market Price: $300-$500/ton

Gross Revenue $3,000-$7,500

Break-even: 5-7 tons/ha

ROI: 150-250% (8-12 month cycle)
