Rice

Oryza sativa

Staple Crop

Origin

Asia (Domesticated 8,000-13,000 years ago)

Yield Potential

2-5 tons/ha

Startup Capital

$1,200 - $2,500/ha

Revenue Potential

$3,000 - $7,500/ha

Importance

Food Security

Rice provides 27% of dietary energy and 20% of dietary protein in West Africa. It's a primary staple for over 50% of the population.

Economic Impact

Rice cultivation supports over 20 million smallholder farmers in West Africa. The regional rice market is valued at over $5 billion annually.

Cultivation Guide

Land Preparation

  1. Clear land of weeds and debris (2-4 weeks before planting)
  2. Plow to 15-20cm depth (wet soils) or 20-25cm (dry soils)
  3. Harrow to break clods and level field for uniform water distribution
  4. Construct bunds (30cm height) for water retention in lowland systems
  5. Apply 5-10 tons/ha of compost or manure (optional but recommended)

Nursery Establishment (for transplanted rice)

  1. Select well-drained site near water source (1/10 of main field area)
  2. Prepare fine seedbed with 2kg seed/m² (upland) or 1kg/m² (lowland)
  3. Sow pre-germinated seeds (soak 24hrs, incubate 24-48hrs)
  4. Maintain shallow water layer (lowland) or frequent irrigation (upland)
  5. Transplant at 3-4 leaf stage (21-30 days after sowing)

Planting

Direct Seeding

  • Seed rate: 80-100kg/ha (drilled) or 120-150kg/ha (broadcast)
  • Row spacing: 20-25cm for mechanized systems
  • Planting depth: 2-3cm

Transplanting

  • Spacing: 20x20cm (fertile soil) to 25x25cm (less fertile)
  • 2-3 seedlings per hill
  • Plant at 3-5cm depth

Maintenance

Water Management

Lowland: Maintain 5-10cm water depth until grain filling. Upland: Irrigate at 40-50% soil moisture depletion.

Weed Control

First weeding at 2-3 weeks, second at 6-7 weeks. Herbicides (pre-emergence) can reduce labor by 60%.

Fertilization

Apply NPK (15-15-15) at 150-300kg/ha in 2-3 splits. Topdress with urea at tillering and panicle initiation.

Harvest & Post-Harvest

Harvesting

  • Timing: 30-35% grain moisture (grains hard, 80-85% panicles yellow)
  • Methods: Manual (sickles) or combine harvesters
  • Yield: 2-3 tons/ha (rainfed), 4-5 tons/ha (irrigated)

Post-Harvest

  • Threshing: Manual or mechanical within 24hrs of harvest
  • Drying: Reduce to 14% moisture (3-5 days sun drying)
  • Storage: Hermetic bags prevent insect damage (0.5-1% loss/month)
  • Milling: 65-70% milling recovery for local varieties
